JUDO

Traditional Japanese Martial Art — Throws, Takedowns & Ground Control

THE ART OF JUDO

Judo — meaning "gentle way" in Japanese — is a dynamic combat sport and martial art developed in Japan in 1882 by Dr. Jigoro Kano. Unlike striking arts, Judo emphasizes throws and takedowns to bring an opponent to the ground, followed by ground control, pins, and submission techniques.

At ZEN BJJ, our Judo program is taught by Wen Hsieh (Judo Black Belt with 17 years of experience) and Mike Ramos (Judo Black Belt and two-time US Olympic Judo Coach). Students benefit from genuinely elite-level instruction rarely found outside major metropolitan areas.

HOW JUDO COMPLEMENTS BJJ & WRESTLING: Judo's throwing techniques are considered among the most sophisticated takedown systems in all of martial arts. For BJJ practitioners, Judo dramatically improves the ability to initiate ground fighting from a position of advantage.

CLASS STRUCTURE

A typical Judo class follows a traditional format that builds technique, strength, and sport-specific fitness.

01

UKEMI (BREAKFALLS)

Every class begins with ukemi — the art of falling safely. Proper breakfall technique is fundamental to Judo training and prevents injury during throws.

02

TACHI-WAZA (THROWS)

The heart of Judo — standing throwing techniques including hip throws (o-goshi), shoulder throws (seoi-nage), leg sweeps (de-ashi-barai), and more. 67 official Judo throws are recognized.

03

NE-WAZA (GROUND)

Ground grappling including osae-komi (pinning), kansetsu-waza (joint locks), and shime-waza (chokes). Ne-waza bridges Judo and BJJ beautifully.

04

RANDORI (SPARRING)

Free practice where students apply techniques against resisting partners in a safe, supervised environment. Randori is where real Judo skills are developed and refined.

We have separate beginner classes for different age groups to ensure appropriate pairing and safe training environments. More advanced students may train together at times, always under careful supervision from our experienced coaches who prioritize safety above all else.
Judo is practiced in a Judogi (Judo uniform), which is heavier and more durable than a standard BJJ Gi. For your very first trial class, any athletic wear or existing BJJ Gi is perfectly fine. We can recommend where to purchase an affordable Judogi if you decide to continue training.
